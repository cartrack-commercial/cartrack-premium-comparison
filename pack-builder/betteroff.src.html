Why it looks more expensive

1
The entire increase is Sasria — and Sasria is not Western's price. Sasria (riot & strike cover) is set by a government pool at a fixed rate on the declared building value; every insurer charges the same rate. Western's schedule declares a slightly larger base (it builds in 10% escalation + 10% inflation), so the Sasria line runs R5,947.85 vs OUTsurance's R3,290.02 — +R2,657.83. That single pass-through is bigger than the whole R1,293.63 increase.
2
Strip Sasria out and Western is cheaper. On the cover Western actually prices — buildings, fire, theft, accidental damage, fidelity, liability — they come in at R7,769.91 vs OUTsurance's R9,134.11. That's R1,364.20/month less for the underwriting itself.
✓

Where the client is genuinely better off

✓
A brand-new R25m umbrella on top of the liability. OUTsurance gave R20m public liability and no umbrella. Western gives R10m public liability plus a R25m umbrella layer — a combined R35m liability tower. That's R15m more protection for a body corporate.
✓
Power-surge cover more than doubled. Upgraded from R108,000 to R270,000 — real protection for a building running solar and electronics.
✓
Higher fidelity cover, for less. Fidelity (trustee / managing-agent dishonesty) rises to R750,000 and the premium drops from R556.59 to R108.33.
✓
Lower excesses and one clean policy. General claims carry a R500 first amount, and the whole book moves to a single insurer, wording and renewal — simpler claims, one point of contact.
Be upfront — two things to close before binding: (1) Western's BAR schedule lists only R50k security equipment, so confirm the ~R4.86m solar is scheduled or add it. (2) Ask Western to re-base the Sasria to the true building value — if it lands nearer OUTsurance's Sasria, this building becomes cheaper and better.
Bottom line

The client pays R1,294 more a month, but every rand of it is the Sasria pass-through. On the cover Western underwrites they're R1,364 cheaper — and they add a R25m umbrella, double the power-surge limit and higher fidelity. Fix the Sasria base and confirm the solar, and 39 Empire is a straight upgrade.

Why it's a bit more expensive

1
Western rates this small residential block a touch higher. On the same R5m building the buildings premium is R752.10 vs Bryte's R366.07 (+R386). This is a small concrete HOA — Western's book prices it up. This is the one number to push back on.
2
Two covers that were "bundled" are now spelled out and priced. Access control (R180,000) is now a scheduled, separately-insured item (+R300) and Fidelity is a stated R250,000 section (+R150) — instead of sitting invisibly inside the building section. You're paying for cover that is now certain rather than assumed.
3
And a lot came down. Sasria (−R324), umbrella (−R65), business all risks (−R83) and the broker fee (−R96 → gone) all dropped — which is why the net increase is only R226, not R836.
✓

Where the client is genuinely better off

✓
The excess on the most likely claim drops from R10,000 to R500. For a residential HOA the number-one claim is water damage / burst pipes. Today a member carries a R10,000 first amount; on Western it's R500. One water claim recovers roughly three-and-a-half years of the premium difference — in the members' pockets, not the insurer's.
✓
Access control is now actually insured. The R180,000 access system is scheduled and named — if it's damaged or fails after an insured event, there's a clear, agreed limit to claim against.
✓
Fidelity is a real, stated section. Trustee / managing-agent dishonesty is covered to R250,000 explicitly — important protection for a self-managed HOA's funds.
✓
Same R25m umbrella, cheaper — and no broker fee. The R25m liability tower stays, at a lower cost, on one consolidated policy.
Be upfront: the building itself is rated higher for the same R5m cover — ask Western to sharpen that buildings rate. If they do, the increase shrinks further while keeping every one of the benefits above.
Bottom line

Winston costs R226 more a month — but it turns a R10,000 excess into R500 on the claim members are most likely to make, puts a named limit on the access system, and makes fidelity a real section. That is a better place for the body corporate to be, even at a slightly higher premium. Push the buildings rate and it's better still.
